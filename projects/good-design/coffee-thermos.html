Three Design Objects  | Coffee Thermos

General description

This is a simple stainless-steel thermos with a detachable lid which I found it quite useful and nice-looking. But this time I have got a few more details that explain why I consider this coffee themos is a good-designed one:

Safely measures

Other than that, I would say the lid of the bottle is also well-designed in a way that helps us consume the hot drink more safely and easily. As you can observe from the picture, the mouth of the bottle has a protruding shape which we can fit our mouth (lips).

And as you further observe from the picture, there is also a small indentation within the tall protruding mouth. I believe that a small indentation would give us better control over how much we would like to drink at once.

Lock of the bottle

There are two separate locks on the lid, one can be flipped, the other can be pressed, this double lock design can better aviod accidential pouring of liquid. The flipping part as well as the botton on the lock have small shape design that helps user to pick and press the lock without much force.

Base of the bottle

Despite having no little pins nor a plastic base that "sticks" the bottle to the surface, the rounded convex with a much heavier base still keeps the bottle steady on the table most of the time.

Other than that, as the base is the heaviest part of the bottle and the gripping part of the bottle is on the upper part, we can check how much liquid is in the bottle by simply slightly sharking the bottle (I don't know is it intended or not).
